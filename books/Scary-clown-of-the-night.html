Scary clown of the night

Author: C. Night

Category: Fiction

Chapter 1: The Carnival Returns

Every year, when autumn settled and the fog thickened over Hollow Creek, an old carnival rolled into town without warning. Its cracked wooden signs swayed eerily, its tents patched with mismatched fabrics, and its lights flickered as if powered by ghosts. This year, however, something felt different. The air was colder, heavier. Children whispered about a clown they saw near the forest's edge—one with a painted smile too wide and eyes that glowed faintly in the dark.

Mila, curious and fearless, stepped closer than most dared. She felt the carnival calling to her, whispering through the wind.

Chapter 2: The Painted Smile

On opening night, Mila wandered through the grounds, drawn by soft carnival music that seemed slightly distorted. As she passed the funhouse, she saw him—the clown. His suit was torn, stained with dirt and old paint. His smile was painted in dripping red, and his eyes glimmered like pale lanterns.

He didn’t speak. He simply waved slowly, each motion unnervingly deliberate. Mila shivered, but something in his expression beckoned her to follow.

Chapter 3: The House of Mirrors

The clown led her to the House of Mirrors. Inside, the reflections twisted unnaturally. Some mirrors showed her frowning when she was smiling. Others stretched her limbs like strings. But one mirror didn’t reflect her at all—it reflected the clown standing behind her, though she could swear he wasn’t there a moment ago.

When she spun around, the room was empty. The lights flickered, and the mirrors whispered her name.

Chapter 4: The Midnight Show

Outside, the carnival had gone silent. Mila wandered into the main tent, where a single spotlight illuminated the dusty stage. The air grew icy. Slowly, the clown stepped into the light, his painted grin wider than before.

“Tonight’s show,” he said in a raspy whisper, “is just for you.”

The seats filled with shadows—barely shaped figures who watched without eyes. Mila’s pulse quickened as the clown juggled objects she couldn’t identify… until she realized they were masks, each one with a frozen scream.

Chapter 5: Secrets of the Carnival

An old fortune teller appeared behind Mila, gripping her wrist. “You shouldn’t be here,” she hissed. “This carnival feeds on fear. That clown? He was once human. Trapped by the carnival’s curse. Each year he seeks a soul brave enough—or foolish enough—to challenge it.”

Mila’s breath caught. “Challenge… what?”

“The heart of the carnival,” the fortune teller said. “Break it, and free us all.”

Chapter 6: The Clown’s Game

Suddenly, Mila was back on the stage, the clown inching toward her. “Let’s play,” he whispered. The tent warped around them, turning into a nightmarish maze filled with laughter that wasn’t laughter at all—more like screams wrapped in joy.

The clown hunted her through the maze, his footsteps echoing unnaturally loud. Mila found broken carnival props scattered around: puppets with missing faces, cracked balloons leaking black mist, and footprints that appeared and vanished at random.

Chapter 7: The Heart of the Night

At the maze’s center, Mila found a carousel made of bones and rusted metal. Its music played backward, each note sharp and painful. Hanging from its center pole was a beating heart—large, shadowy, pulsing with the carnival’s energy.

The clown leapt onto the platform, blocking her path. “Break it,” he snarled, “and we vanish forever. Some of us don’t want to go.”

Mila swallowed her fear and stepped forward.

Chapter 8: The Final Laugh

The heart throbbed violently as she touched it, showing flashes of the clown’s past—his human face, his laughter, his fall into darkness. He lunged in desperation, his painted smile trembling. Mila struck the heart with a shattered piece of mirror, and a scream—too loud to be earthly—echoed through the carnival.

The tents collapsed, lights exploded into sparks, and the clown’s figure dissolved into drifting dust. For the first time, he smiled without paint—grateful, free.

Chapter 9: Dawn Over Hollow Creek

Mila awoke in the empty field where the carnival once stood. Only trampled grass and the faint smell of popcorn remained. No tents. No mirrors. No clown. The sun rose slowly, burning away the mist.

The town never believed her story, but they noticed something: the carnival never returned. The fog lifted earlier each year. And every so often, Mila felt a warm breeze at night—as if someone was thanking her.

Chapter 10: The Quiet Laughter

Years passed. Mila grew older, but she never forgot the painted smile or the shadowed maze. On certain nights, under a pale moon, she heard soft laughter—not frightening, but familiar. Gentle. Free.

And she knew the spirit of the clown was finally at peace, watching over the very place that once trapped him in darkness.
